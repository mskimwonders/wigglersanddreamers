About Kim

I'm a PK-8 STEM Coordinator at Prospect Hill Academy Charter School, a diverse urban district where 75% of students are multilingual learners. My work focuses on creating inclusive, rigorous STEM experiences that honor the full range of how students learn and think.

Before this role, I worked at Acera School, where most students were twice exceptional with high rates of ADHD and autism. That experience fundamentally shaped how I think about curriculum design, assessment, and what it means to position all students as mathematicians and scientists.

School should be a place where every kind of person‚Äîwith every kind of mind‚Äîcan belong and thrive. Learning is the most marvelous kind of fun, and kids' brains want nothing more than to love, be loved, and to learn. If kids don't want to do those things at school, it's school that's the problem, not them.

I'm passionate about making math education more playful, accessible, and affirming‚Äîespecially for students whose brains work differently. The strategies I share aren't just accommodations; they're often better pedagogy for everyone.
